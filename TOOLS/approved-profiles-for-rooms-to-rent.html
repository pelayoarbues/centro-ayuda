Approved profiles for rooms to rent

If you publish a room to rent and someone contacts you in relation to that listing, you can learn some useful information about the candidate. This happens when the person has decided to share their profile with you when they contact you. Their information will be provided in the form of a file with information of interest, and their preferences, which will help you to know if it is a suitable profile for the owner.

Where can you see the requests to rent rooms?

These requests for information arrive with the rest of the listings to the Inbox. From here you can differentiate between the contacts with profiles and without, and thereby manage them easier.
Those who choose to share their profile and are verified by idealista will appear in the Inbox menu as “approved profile”:

Los que tienen esta etiqueta además de compartir su perfil para búsqueda de habitaciones, son usuarios verificados. Es decir, por parte de idealista comprobamos que es un mail de uso habitual y no es falso.
As well as sharing their profile to search for rooms, those with this tag are verified users. This means that idealista tests that it is an email in use, and not a fake account.

What information can I know about the interested person?
• Their age and gender
• If they are working / studying
• If they smoke at home or not
• If they have a pet
• If they are looking for a room for themselves or for other people as well
• The mínimum stay
• Their social media profiles

What happens if I want to turn this conversation into an application?
Now that idealista/tools no longer offers the possibility of creating an applicant for a room to rent, so long as they do not ask about any other property we will be referring to a contact and not an applicant.
Now, suppose the same person enters into contact again, but this time regarding a studio, or another type of property that is considered by application: in this case we consider the first person on the advertisement as a more pressing lead to follow up on. You will no longer see them as an approved contact, now that the priority is having them as an applicant for a complete home.
